~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~    Common PAGE 3 Notes    ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
Site preparation, construction materials and installation to in accordance with Section 11 of the Maine Subsurface Wastewater Disposal Rules (August 3, 2015).
Septic tank setback to foundation is min. 8 feet. Disposal field to foundation min. setback is 20 feet to full or frost wall foundation, and 15 feet to a slab or column foundation.
Site Evaluator assumes building sewer will be at an elevation to allow gravity flow from septic tank to the disposal field, therefore a pump is not required.

Insulate the Distribution Box (D-Box).

Min. 1/4"/ft (2%) pitch of pipe from building to septic tank.
Min. 1/8"/ft (1%) pitch of pipe from septic tank to disposal field.

If a pump station is not required then a 1/8"/ft (1%) 4" dia. Solid PVC pipe to be used between septic tank and disposal field.
If a pump station is required then a 1.5" or 2" dia. force main shall be installed between the pump station and D-box.  Force main to enter bottom of the distribution box.

Force main to pitch towards pump station, such that it will completely drain following each pump cycle. Drill 1/4" hole in bottom of force main between the inside of the pump chamber and the check valve to allow force main to drain following each dosing cycle.  Force main to enter into bottom of distribution box (D-box).

This design complies with and must be installed in accordance with the Eljen Design and Installation Manual.  If greater than 18 inches fill above GSF Modules then venting is required.  This system is not designed to be used with a garbage disposal or for backwash from a water softner.
